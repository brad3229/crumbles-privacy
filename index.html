Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 19, 2026
Your privacy matters to us. Crumbles is designed to help you track your own receipts and expenses. We collect only what we need to make the app work, and we never sell your data.

1. Information We Collect

We collect the following information when you use Crumbles:

  • Account information: Your name and email address, provided when you sign up or sign in with Google.
  • Receipt data: Merchant name, amount, date, category, payment method, tax amount, warranty dates, notes, store location, invoice number, and store phone number — entered manually or extracted from receipt photos.
  • Receipt images: Photos you take or upload of your receipts, stored securely in the cloud.
  • API key: Your Claude AI (Anthropic) API key, if you choose to provide one for AI receipt scanning. This is stored encrypted in our database.
  • Preferences: App settings such as theme (light/dark) and language preference, stored locally on your device.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide and operate the Crumbles app and its features.
  • Sync your receipts and data across your devices.
  • Process receipt images through AI to automatically extract information (Pro users only, using your own API key).
  • Send local notifications for warranty expiration reminders.
  • Generate spending summaries, tax reports, and CSV exports for your personal use.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose beyond operating the app.

3. Third-Party Services

Crumbles uses the following third-party services to operate:

  • Supabase — Our database and authentication provider. Your account information, receipt data, and images are stored on Supabase servers. Supabase is SOC 2 compliant. Supabase Privacy Policy
  • Anthropic (Claude AI) — If you use AI receipt scanning, your receipt images are sent to Anthropic's API for processing. This uses your own API key. Images are processed and not stored by Anthropic for training. Anthropic Privacy Policy
  • Google Sign-In — If you choose to sign in with Google, your name and email are shared with us by Google. Google Privacy Policy

4. Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored securely using Supabase, which provides encrypted storage and row-level security (RLS) to ensure only you can access your own data. Your Claude API key is stored securely and never exposed publicly.

Receipt images are stored in a secure cloud storage bucket with access restricted to your account only.

5. Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, your data will be removed from our servers within 30 days. You can delete individual receipts at any time within the app.

6. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correct inaccurate data within the app.
  • Delete your account and all associated data.
  • Export your receipt data as a CSV at any time using the Export screen.

To request account deletion or a copy of your data, contact us at the email below.

7. Children's Privacy

Crumbles is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

8. Local Device Storage

In addition to cloud storage, Crumbles stores certain preferences locally on your device (such as theme and language settings) using AsyncStorage. This data never leaves your device.

9. Notifications

Crumbles may send you local push notifications on your device to remind you about upcoming warranty expirations. These notifications are generated locally on your device and do not involve sending data to any server. You can disable notifications at any time in your device settings.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
